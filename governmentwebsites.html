Montana

Montana is the first state website that I reviewed. At first glance it seems kind of bland, but there is lots of info right as you enter the website. I mean that you pretty much don't have to scroll to find anything. There is a bunch of links at the top of the website, there are links to resources, and news releases, as well as information on the governor. But to add onto all that, there is more information when you scroll down. They have an interactive map, and information on all of the representatives, and senators for the state. There is a photo gallery, and then at the bottom of the page there are some links, and there are also some facts about Montana, like the state bird, motto, and animal. Just some neat information. Overall, I was impressed with Montana's website, and I was honestly kind of suprised. I thought it was going to be super outdated or something, but it was actually the best of the five that I looked at by far, which again, really suprised me.

California

The second best website that I looked at. It's design is pretty similar to most state websites, or all good informational websites really. It's got all those links at the top of the page, and a bunch more resources. The color scheme is good, and theres a bunch more information and resources further down. But, it dosen't have as much stuff as Montana's website.

Hawaii

Hawaii is the thrid ranked of the websites that I looked at. I thought about putting it at second, but I personally like the way Cali, and Montana have their set up more, with that header of links they have at the top of their pages. Hawaii's website is still nice though. There is lots of information available on it. They even have the weather. Further down on the website there are sections dedicated to the government, business, residents, and visitors. In each section are links that are specifically for those people. I think that is interesting, and well designed. I also like the more colorful look of the website.

Arizona

Arizon is ranked fourth. Arizone is a pretty simple website. It has a few links at the top of the screen, and it is also formatted differently. You don't scroll down, but there is a kind of slide show type thing instead, where each slide is a link. But overall there isn't much on this website. So, it gets 4th.

Florida

Florida's website is super simple, but there isn't much on it. There is a search bar right at the front of the page, as well as a few links at the top right. There are also a few more links lower on the page, but overall this website is super empty. It feels like they started it and then just decided they didn't want to complete it. Because of that it gets 5th.
